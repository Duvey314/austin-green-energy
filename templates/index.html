About The Project

_ _ _

The Austin Energy Predictor can be used to determine energy output in Megawatt-Hours (MWH) per renewable energy farm, specifically wind and solar farms. Our team collaborated with Austin Energy, a publicly owned utility that provides electrical power to the city of Austin and other surrounding areas in Texas. This project uses 2017-2020 hourly megawatt data from Austin Energy’s farms: Hackberry Wind Farm and Webberville Solar Farm as well as hourly historical weather data to train a machine learning model.

The combination of MWH output and weather data allows the model to predict energy output based on weather factors - such as temperature, wind speed and direction, and cloud coverage. As the model was trained on three years’ data, the model comprises weather variations that are observed across different seasons. We were able to take January-July 2020 output and weather data for the above farms to test the model and evaluate its accuracy.

The data insights generated by the model are vital since renewable energy generation varies continuously throughout the day and based on available wind and sunshine. We hope to utilize the model on all of Austin Energy’s renewable energy farms and expand the model to include peak power generation times. With this model, Austin Energy will have a more refined understanding of its overall energy output and when renewable energy generated will be at its highest.

Austin Green Energy Predictor

Welcome to the Austin Energy Predictor! The predictor uses forecasted weather data, historical energy output data, and a neural network machine learning model to make its predictions. Use the predictor to generate the predicted energy output (megawatt-hour, Mwh) for the Hackberry Wind Farm and Webberville Solar Farm for the next 48 hours.

Model Details

Please select a date using the date picker and press the Show Data button. Two graphs will display showing the predicted output from our model and actual output from the historical data. Hover over an data point to see more information.
